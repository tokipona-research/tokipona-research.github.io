Hypothesis

Learning Toki Pona and achieving proficiency and/or fluency can improve the following in students:

  • Reading comprehension
  • Pattern recognition
  • Mathematical skills
  • General English skills

Proposed Methodology

Participants will follow a structured program to learn Toki Pona, a minimalist constructed language developed by linguist Sonja Lang in 2001. Toki Pona has only 120-140 words and over 29,000 known speakers worldwide.

The course will provide learning resources assembled to progress the learner from no knowledge to conversational fluency. There will be 30-minute to one-hour learning sessions every weekday (Monday through Friday) and weekly assessments.

This study will take 4-6 weeks. The target sample size is 15 participants per grade with a control group of 6 per grade.

Learning Resources

  • lipu sona - A Toki Pona crash course spanning twenty lessons with vocabulary and grammar
  • o pilin e toki pona series by jan Telakoman - Video series to enhance speech, listening, and fluency

Proficiency Scores

Participants' proficiency scores will be defined with the formula: p = (v + g) / 2, where:

  • v = % of Toki Pona Vocabulary learned
  • g = grammatical accuracy %
  • p = proficiency score (0-100)
Proficiency Score Category Ranking
0-20 beginner
21-40 novice
41-60 intermediate
61-80 proficient
81-100 conversational

Privacy and Safety

  • Voluntary participation: All participation is 100% voluntary
  • Parental consent: Required for all participants
  • Complete anonymity: Only proficiency statistics, grade level, and languages spoken are collected

Publication and Data Rights

As the primary investigator and designer of this study, I, Noah Fountain, will retain the right to be the sole author of any formal publication or public presentation of the research findings. Data will be used exclusively for this study and its subsequent analysis. Acknowledgments will be provided to all who assist.
